Stack

SUPPLEMENTS

Your full stack optimised for timing. Ashwagandha, creatine, plant protein, multivitamin, calcium, magnesium — all timed for maximum absorption alongside your night-shift schedule.

🌿
Ashwagandha (KSM-66)
As prescribed · typically 300–600mg
Before bedor with dinner
Adaptogen — reduces cortisol, improves recovery, boosts testosterone naturally. Critical for night shift workers whose cortisol is chronically elevated. Take at night for best effect. Known to improve sleep quality and reduce gym recovery time.
⚡
Creatine Monohydrate
4g daily — every single day
Post-gym shakeWith breakfast (rest days)
At 4g/day you are perfectly dosed. Mix into your post-gym plant protein shake. On rest days take with breakfast. Consistency matters more than timing. Improves strength output by 5–15% and helps with muscle volumisation. No loading needed.
🌱
Plant Protein
2 scoops · 50g protein daily
Post-gym — primary doseOptional 1 scoop breakfast
2 scoops post-gym = 50g protein in one hit — this is your most important supplement window. Ensure your plant protein has a complete amino acid profile (pea + rice blend is best). If leucine content is low, consider adding 2–3g leucine separately to maximise muscle protein synthesis.
🌟
Multivitamin
1 tablet daily
With breakfast (11 AM)
Take with food for fat-soluble vitamin absorption (A, D, E, K). Night shift workers are almost universally deficient in Vitamin D — check if your multi has 1000+ IU D3. If not, add a separate D3 supplement (2000 IU daily). This alone will improve mood, testosterone, and recovery.
🦴
Calcium
As prescribed · 500–1000mg
With lunch or dinnerNOT with iron foods
Critical for muscle contraction and bone density under heavy training load. Do NOT take with iron-rich foods — they compete for absorption. Space 2 hours from multivitamin if it contains iron. If on 1000mg, split into two 500mg doses for better absorption.
🌙
Magnesium
As prescribed · 200–400mg
Always before bed
Your single most impactful supplement for a night-shift athlete. Improves sleep depth, reduces muscle cramps, lowers cortisol, aids recovery. Magnesium glycinate or citrate absorbs best. Take 30–60 minutes before sleep. You should notice better sleep quality within 1–2 weeks.

Cardiovascular

CARDIO PLAN

Programmed to maximise fat loss without eating muscle. Phases escalate gradually. LISS protects muscle. HIIT accelerates fat loss. Treadmill, bike, jump rope, and elliptical rotated to prevent adaptation. Never cardio after legs day.

Golden Rules
🫀
LISS Heart Rate
120–140 BPM. Fat-burning zone. Can hold a conversation. Preserves muscle while burning fat.
⚡
HIIT Structure
20–30s all-out : 40–60s rest. 15–20 mins total only. Never on leg day or the day after.
🍌
Pre-Cardio Fuel
Never do HIIT fasted. Banana or dates 30 min before. LISS can be done fasted if preferred.
🦵
Legs Day Rule
Zero cardio on Friday legs day. Your legs need every bit of recovery available.

Cable Crunch (Kneeling)
Upper Abs · Weighted
Kneel at cable, rope behind head, crunch till elbows touch knees. 3s eccentric on the way up. Best weighted ab exercise. Add weight every 2 weeks.
3–4 × 12–15 · progressive weight
Hanging Leg Raise
Lower Abs · Hard
Dead hang, raise straight legs to 90°. No swinging. Start bent-knee version. Progress to toes-to-bar by Phase 3.
3–4 × 10–12 reps
Ab Wheel Rollout
Full Core · Advanced
From knees, roll out as far as possible without hips sagging. Pause at full extension. Pull back using abs. One of the best total core exercises.
3 × 10–15 reps
Pallof Press (Cable)
Anti-Rotation · Functional
Stand side-on to cable, press handle straight out, hold 2s, return. Don't rotate. Anti-rotation challenge is the entire point.
3 × 12–15 each side
Dead Bug
Anti-Extension · Safe
Lie on back, arms up, knees 90°. Lower opposite arm and leg simultaneously. Keep lower back flat. Best core stabilisation exercise — looks easy, isn't.
3 × 10 each side
Copenhagen Plank
Obliques · Hip Adductors
Side plank with top foot on bench. Works obliques and hip adductors — directly addresses your under-developed leg/hip segment flagged on InBody.
3 × 20–35s each side
V-Ups
Full Abs · Dynamic
Simultaneously raise legs and torso to touch hands to feet. Control descent. Keep feet off ground between reps for constant tension.
3 × 15 reps
Dragon Flag (Phase 3)
Advanced · Full Core
On bench, grip behind head. Raise entire body in straight line. Lower slowly — this is the hard part. Start with negatives only. Brucelee-approved.
3–4 × 5–8 negatives
Hollow Body Hold
Full Core · Isometric
Lower back pressed into floor, arms overhead, legs at 30–45°, hold. Everything tight. Add rocking for extra challenge.
3–4 × 30–45s holds
Side Plank + Hip Dip
Obliques · Lateral Core
Side plank position. Dip hip to floor, raise back to alignment. Controlled movement. Great for lateral core which is often undertrained.
3 × 12 dips each side

Coach Notes — Read Before Day 1
01Legs are the mission. Both flagged Under on InBody segmental. Treat Friday like the most important session of the week. Never skip it. Never replace with cardio.
02Plant protein 2 scoops post-gym = 50g. This is your anabolic window. Never skip it. If your plant protein is pea-only, add 2–3g leucine for better muscle protein synthesis.
03Technique key. SS = Superset · RP = Rest-Pause (10 breaths, continue) · AMRAP = As Many Reps As Possible · DS = Drop Set (–20% load, continue) · Mech Drop = Mechanical Drop Set (change angle, same weight) · F = To Failure · p = Partial reps.
04Night shift optimization. Pre-gym at 2:30 PM = biggest carb meal. Post-gym shake within 30 min. Avoid heavy carbs after midnight. Magnesium before sleep every night without exception.
05Three InBody checkpoints. End of Week 4, Week 8, Day 90. Same time of day and hydration state each time. These are your scoreboard — trust the process between scans.
